I grew up in this area. I watched council tax go up year after year while the roads fell apart and nobody at County Hall could explain where the money was going. I stopped waiting for someone else to fix it.

In May 2025, Lancashire voted for change. Reform won 53 of 84 seats, the largest majority any party has held at County Hall. As Lead Member for Finance and Resources, we inherited a £28 million overspend, £350 million in concealed bond losses, and a savings programme delivering at just 48%. In our first budget, we delivered the lowest council tax rise in over a decade, balanced the books, and kept all five county care homes open against the national trend.

I dig into the numbers and I ask the questions that need asking. The people paying the bills have a right to know where their money goes.

Key Milestones

May 2025

Elected to Lancashire County Council

Won Padiham and Burnley West for Reform UK as part of a landslide: 53 of 84 seats, the largest majority any party has held at County Hall.

May 2025

Appointed to Cabinet

Appointed Lead Member for Finance and Resources, taking on oversight of the council's £986 million net revenue budget and financial strategy.

Summer 2025

Five Care Homes Saved

Launched an eight-week public consultation on the future of five county care homes. 1,600 residents responded. We listened, and kept all five open. Nationally, council-run care home beds have fallen from 64% to just 4% since 1979.

Autumn 2025

£350M Bond Losses Uncovered

Reform's financial scrutiny of the Statement of Accounts revealed that £519 million invested in VeLTIP bonds had fallen to just £169 million. The previous administration never transparently reported these losses to councillors or the public.

February 2026

First Reform Budget

Balanced a £1.33 billion budget with the lowest council tax rise in over a decade at 3.80%, down from 4.99% in each of the last two Conservative budgets. The inherited £28 million overspend corrected without cutting front-line services. We are stopping the rot.

Reform at Lancashire County Council

When the Conservatives left County Hall, they left behind a mess: a £28 million overspend, savings delivery that had collapsed to just 48%, council tax raised to the legal maximum in each of their final two budgets, and £350 million in bond losses they never transparently reported.

In less than a year, Reform has delivered the lowest council tax rise in over a decade, corrected the inherited overspend, saved all five county care homes, and stopped councillors collecting salaries for pension fund directorships. We identified £5 in every £100 of council spending as potential savings. The 2026/27 budget is balanced at £1.33 billion, without cutting front-line services.
