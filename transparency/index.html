What is AI DOGE?

AI DOGE is a free, independent platform that analyses public spending data from all 15 Lancashire councils, from small district councils like Ribble Valley to the county council itself and the two unitary authorities, Blackpool and Blackburn with Darwen. Every transaction is searchable, every supplier is tracked, and every pattern is flagged.

The platform applies 35+ forensic techniques drawn from professional audit standards (ACFE, INTOSAI, Nigrini). These include duplicate payment detection, split payment evasion analysis, Benford's Law screening of payment distributions, supplier concentration scoring using the Herfindahl-Hirschman Index (HHI), year-end spike detection, round-number anomaly checks, payment cadence analysis, and weak competition flagging.

Anyone can search every transaction, explore DOGE forensic findings, review councillor integrity data including Companies House directorships and Electoral Commission donations, and examine budget breakdowns sourced directly from GOV.UK outturn data. The platform also tracks procurement notices, council tax collection rates, and ward-level deprivation data.

AI DOGE is not affiliated with any council or political party. It is a grassroots transparency tool, built and maintained by me because I believe residents deserve to see exactly where their money goes, and to hold decision-makers to account with hard data, not just rhetoric.

Making a Freedom of Information Request

The Freedom of Information Act 2000 gives you the right to request any recorded information held by a public authority in England, Wales, and Northern Ireland. This includes councils, NHS bodies, police forces, schools, and central government departments.

You do not need to give a reason for your request. The authority must respond within 20 working days, and can only refuse on specific legal grounds (such as national security, personal data, or commercial confidentiality). If they refuse, they must explain why and tell you how to appeal.

AI DOGE already makes a significant amount of council spending data freely available, often in far more detail than councils publish themselves. Before submitting an FOI request, it is worth checking AI DOGE to see whether the information you need is already there. If it is not, an FOI request is your legal right and a powerful tool for holding councils to account.

To make a request to Lancashire County Council, you can use their online FOI portal or write to their information governance team directly.
